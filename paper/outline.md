# The Diffusion State: China’s AI Industrialization Model and the Next Productivity Shock

**Target:** NBER Economics of AI conference (papers due 4 June 2026).

**Empirical status:** Measurement and disciplined descriptive / quasi-experimental evidence only. No causal claims until city controls and robustness blocks are complete.

---

## 1. Motivation

China’s AI policy stack combines national pilot-zone designation, smart-manufacturing recognition, and export-oriented industry. The paper asks whether this bundle constitutes a distinctive **diffusion state**—institutional capacity to move AI from rhetoric into listed industrial adoption—rather than only frontier-model capability.

## 2. Contribution (as of pipeline v1)

1. **Reproducible measurement** of national AI pilot zones (17 units, 2019–2021) linked to MIIT excellence-level smart-factory lists (509 projects, 2024–2025).
2. **Transparent geo and industry coding** with confidence flags; province-only list locations are not imputed to cities.
3. **Descriptive and associational evidence** on pilot-zone overlap and post-designation adoption patterns, with an explicit red-team memo on identification limits.

## 3. Empirical chain

```text
AI pilot zones → smart-factory recognition → (planned) industrial AI diffusion → export upgrading
```

**Built in repo:** pilot zones, smart factories, BACI sector exports, city-year panels, Tables 1–3, event study, optional Table 4.

**Blocked on external data:** EPS/NBS city controls (`make city-controls`).
